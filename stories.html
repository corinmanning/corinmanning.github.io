Female Athletes that have torn their ACL

Candace Parker

On July 11, 2003, Parker tore her ACL in her left knee in a summer league game. On December 29, Parker returned to action for Naperville Central and a few months later, led her team to its second consecutive state title.

Alex Morgan

Ten years ago, American soccer player Alex Morgan suffered a torn anterior cruciate ligament (ACL). After surgery, she resumed a successful career. In the 2012 London Olympics, she scored the winning goal against Canada to send the U.S. to the gold-medal game. There, she faced off with Japan and the U.S. won.

Caroline Doty

Her contribution to Connecticut in 2011 was limited to suggesting plays from the sidelines. Doty first tore her ACL while playing soccer as a high school senior in the fall of 2007. On January 27, 2009, during her freshman season against Syracuse, she drove for a layup and landed awkwardly. In August of 2010, while practicing one-on-one moves, she drove for a left-handed layup and felt her left knee shift when she landed. The ACL was 80 percent torn and third operation was required.
